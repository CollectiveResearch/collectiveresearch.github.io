About Us

Collective Research is a community for people who want to contribute to human knowledge in a manner that is accessible, dynamic and verifiable. Many of us also participate in open science, open access, citizen science, participatory medicine, and free/libre/open source software communities. Our first (but not only) project, temporarily titled ResearchMods, is currently in the beginning stages of planning.

We started in January 2015, so we're still pretty new! To learn more about us, join the discussion or start contributing.

Contribute

We're literally just getting started. Contribution right now means joining the discussion about how to build our tools and community. You can also join us on IRC, channel #collectiveresearch on Freenode. (Not familiar with IRC? Try webchat.)

Mission Statement

The values that drive our work.

Accessible

You can't have accessibility without access, but true openness is so much more than sharing your code and data. Mentoring, documentation, and user support are valued just as highly here as coding and data science. A welcoming community is also vital for accessibility, so we have a Code of Conduct and abide by the Hacker School social rules.

Dynamic

What we know is always changing, and our approach to research reflects that dynamism. We build tools which embrace change on every level, tools which are themselves easily updated, remixed and shared.

Verifiable

It's vital that we be able to trust our collective knowledge, including our knowledge of what is unknown and what is uncertain. We embrace automated testing and personal verification. Our community encourages questioning and critique, and our tools are designed to help us keep our knowledge base reliable.
